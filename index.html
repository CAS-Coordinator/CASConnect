Spring 2026: Eastern Shizuoka CAS Opportunities

March and April represent a critical transitional period in Japan. As the academic and fiscal years restart, there is an urgent demand for high school volunteers to support cherry blossom festivals, agricultural transitions, and educational orientations for foreign residents. Explore the curated opportunities across Numazu, Mishima, Fuji City, and the surrounding 20km radius to fulfill your Creativity, Activity, and Service requirements.

Strategic Portfolio Insights

Addressing Demographic Shifts

Local authorities increasingly rely on high school students to maintain civic functions. Treat events like the Ose Festival not as casual charity, but as urgent civic partnerships necessary for regional survival (LO6: Global Engagement).

Overcoming Transport Friction

The 20km radius has limited public transit. Don't hide the logistical struggle! Use the hours spent calculating bus routes to isolated sites like the Toda River Light-up to fulfill LO3 (Initiative and Planning).

Remote Empathy Protocols

Engaging in remote counseling or the ASEAN Youth Summit? Be sure to reflect heavily on the strict digital privacy protocols and cross-cultural boundaries required for these tasks (LO7: Ethical Choices).
